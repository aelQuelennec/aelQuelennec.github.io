Research

The long-term vision driving my PhD is the deployment of adaptive AI models directly on embedded devices such as EEG headsets as enabling real-time, privacy-preserving neural signal processing at the edge represents a significant challenge. Achieving this requires rethinking how neural networks learn under the severe memory and energy constraints of embedded hardware, a challenge that lies at the heart of the green AI agenda. My work tackles this problem along three complementary directions:

‚ö°

Dynamic Subnetwork Selection

Starting from a preliminary exploration of neuron selection based on a Velocity metric, this line of work progressively deepened its theoretical grounding. TraDy emerged from a thorough study of gradient dynamics during training, leveraging heavy-tailed gradient theory to build a principled framework for layer and channel selection under strict memory constraints. Building on this understanding, MeDyate further exploits gradient properties through stochastic channel selection, translating theoretical insight into state-of-the-art fine-tuning performance.

Activation Map Compression

This line of work begins by adapting traditional weight low-rank decomposition strategies to the compression of activation maps stored during backpropagation, the dominant memory bottleneck during training. While HOSVD demonstrates the strong compression potential of this approach, its high decomposition cost makes it impractical for real-world use. ASI addresses this limitation through a shortcut approach that bypasses the computational overhead, achieving comparable memory reduction and accuracy to HOSVD while running faster than standard uncompressed training.

EEG/ECG Signal Acquisition

In parallel to my AI-focused research, I contributed to a group project designing a data collection protocol for EEG and ECG signals during attentional tasks. The protocol investigates three types of sensory cues (visual, auditory and haptic) and involved the full pipeline from material selection, ethical and legal approval to code conception and testing, leaving a protocol ready for deployment.
